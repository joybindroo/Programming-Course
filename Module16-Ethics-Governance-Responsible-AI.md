# Module 16 – Ethics, Governance & Responsible AI

---

## Learning Objectives
- Recognize ethical risks in AI systems (bias, privacy, misuse).
- Apply fairness metrics and mitigation techniques.
- Understand regulatory frameworks (GDPR, EU AI Act, US AI Bill of Rights).
- Design governance processes for model lifecycle management.
- Communicate AI risks and benefits to stakeholders.

## Overview
AI brings powerful capabilities but also societal responsibilities. This module equips engineers with the mindset and tools to develop AI responsibly and transparently.

## Key Topics
1. **Bias & Fairness** – sources of bias, measurement (demographic parity, equalized odds), mitigation (re‑sampling, adversarial debiasing).
2. **Privacy** – differential privacy, data minimization, anonymization.
3. **Explainability** – SHAP, LIME, model cards, datasheets for datasets.
4. **Regulation** – GDPR Article 22, EU AI Act risk categories, US AI Bill of Rights.
5. **Model Governance** – versioning, provenance, audit trails, risk assessments.
6. **Human‑in‑the‑Loop** – oversight, escalation, fallback strategies.
7. **Stakeholder Communication** – impact assessments, transparency reports.
8. **Case Studies** – facial recognition, hiring algorithms, medical diagnostics.

## Mini‑Lab
1. Take a pre‑trained sentiment classifier and evaluate **bias** across gendered pronouns using the **`fairlearn`** toolkit.
2. Apply **differential privacy** to a simple logistic regression model with the **`opacus`** library.
3. Generate a **model card** documenting intended use, limitations, and ethical considerations.
4. Draft a **risk assessment** checklist for deploying the model in a production API.
